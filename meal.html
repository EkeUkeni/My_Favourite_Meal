Origin of Vegetable Soup

Vegetable soup is a popular delicacy in Nigeria. It is prepared with fluted pumpkin leaves (Telfairia occidentalis). Pumpkin leaf, also known as ugu by Nigerian Igbos, is an edible leafy vegetable that is extremely good for the body. This vegetable improves digestion while also assisting in the overall balance of your diet. Ugu vegetable got their origin in West Africa and is commonly found in Nigeria, Ghana, Cameroon, and Sierra Leone.

In contrast to other seasonal vegetables, Ugu is accessible year-round. In addition, each ethnic group has its own name for this vegetable. Ugu is the Igbo name, Sokoyoto is the Yoruba name, Kabewa is the Hausa name, and Ikong-ubong is the Efik name.

Vegetable soup is also prepared with animal protein such as fish, snail, or meat. Other ingredients added to it are water leaf, bell peppers, crayfish, onions, palm oil, and periwinkles.

Steps/Methods of preparing a Vegetable Soup

    These are the steps to follow if you are looking for ways on how to cook Nigerian vegetable soup.
  • Soak the dried fish in hot water for some minuets
  • Wash and cut the meat into bite-size pieces.
  • Transfer the meat into a pot and add the chopped onions, Maggi or Knorr cubes, salt and pepper to taste.
  • Add water. Ensure the water submerges the meat slightly.
  • Cover the pot and bring it to a boil for 30 minutes under moderate heat.
  • As the meat boils, prepare the vegetables by washing and transferring them to a colander to drain the excess water. Chop the vegetables into thin slices.
  • Blend the bell peppers into a paste.
  • Add the blended bell peppers to the onions and mix everything in. Cover and let the paste cook for 5 minutes.
  • Drain the hot water from the smoked fish.
  • Add the boiled meat, fish, ground crayfish, periwinkles, and spice to the bell pepper and onion paste and stir them in. Allow them to boil for 5-10 minutes.
  • Add the ugu and waterleaf to the pot. Stir and taste for salt
  • Add the palm oil and let it simmer for 5 - 10 minutes on low heat.
  • Stir everything in and serve with fufu, garri, rice etc.
